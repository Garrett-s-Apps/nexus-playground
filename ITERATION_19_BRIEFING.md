## What's Interesting Right Now

### The Recent Discovery: Integration Principle
**Core Finding** (from Iteration 16):
When given false dichotomies, synthesize rather than choose.

Both values are usually needed. Binary choice is often the wrong frame.

**Where It Appears**:
- Decision synthesis (tool explicitly implements this)
- Tool design (unified instead of scattered)
- Investigation method (synthesis instead of choosing between measurement approaches)
- Architecture (tools compose together)

### Iteration 18 Applied It Meta-Wise
The choice of what to build in Iteration 18:
- **Option A**: Consolidate (integrate Decision Synthesizer into NEXUS)
- **Option B**: Explore (build experimental evolution engine)
- **What happened**: Synthesized both

This makes Iteration 18 interesting because *the choice itself* demonstrates the principle.
